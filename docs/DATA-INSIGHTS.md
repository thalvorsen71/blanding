## Notable Findings

**Language vs. Strategy gap:** Average language score (54.1) consistently beats strategy score (47.9). Schools are slightly better at not sounding generic than they are at actually differentiating. Writing quality > strategic positioning, across the board.

**The "world-class" problem:** When 1 in 3 schools calls itself "world-class," the word becomes meaningless. It's the higher ed equivalent of every restaurant calling itself "authentic."

**News ≠ differentiation:** Many schools score modestly on specificity (they have real news, named people, dates) but still fail on brand theatre and AI readiness. Having specific content isn't the same as having strategic content. A school can list every Fulbright scholar and still never answer "why here?"

**0 schools above 80:** Not a single school in 205 has cracked the "Standing Out" tier. The ceiling is MIT at 76. This isn't because the scoring is broken. It's because no school's website, as measured by its text alone, is truly distinctive enough that you could identify it without the logo.
