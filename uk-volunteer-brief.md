# Blanding UK Edition: Volunteer Brief

## What This Is

We're building a UK edition of the Blanding Audit, an AI-powered tool that scores how original (or cliché-ridden) a university's website language is. The US version has already analyzed 200+ schools and the data is brutal: not a single school scored above 80/100.

The UK higher ed market has its own version of this problem, but different language patterns. That's where you come in.

We need your knowledge of UK university marketing to build a cliché detection system that's accurate and fair for .ac.uk institutions. **We don't need technical help, design input, or feature ideas.** We need your expertise on the language.

## What We Need From You

### 1. The Cliché List (most important)

Browse 10-15 UK university homepages and primary landing pages (prospectus pages, "Why Choose Us," course listing intros). Write down every phrase that makes you roll your eyes. Not what you think might be cliché. What you've personally seen so many times it's lost all meaning.

**Starter questions to get you going:**

- What's the UK equivalent of "world-class"?
- What phrases appear on every Russell Group site?
- What do post-92 universities say to try to sound like Russell Group ones?
- What language do clearing campaigns all default to?
- What do international student recruitment pages always say?
- What phrases would a prospective student mock or tune out?

**Some US clichés for reference (so you see the format we're after):**

- "World-class" (33% of US schools)
- "Interdisciplinary" (32%)
- "Commitment to excellence"
- "Vibrant community"
- "Cutting-edge research"
- "Holistic approach"
- "Transformative experience"

**We want the UK version of this list.** Include phrases exactly as they appear on sites. The more specific, the better.

### 2. Institutional vs. Marketing Language

UK higher ed has terminology that's genuinely meaningful but could look like filler to an outsider. Help us draw the line.

**Please categorize these types of phrases:**

| Category | What It Means | Example |
|----------|--------------|---------|
| **Legitimate institutional language** | Real designations, frameworks, or metrics that carry actual meaning | REF results, TEF Gold, AACSB accredited |
| **Marketing filler dressed as substance** | Sounds official but says nothing specific | "Research-intensive environment," "sector-leading teaching quality" |
| **Pure cliché** | Meaningless on its own, used by everyone | "Vibrant campus life," "globally recognised" |

If there are phrases you're unsure about, flag them. That uncertainty is useful data.

### 3. What Pages Matter

US .edu sites follow a fairly predictable structure. UK university sites may differ.

Tell us: **What are the 3-5 pages a prospective undergraduate actually reads when deciding?** Homepage is obvious. What else? Course pages? Student life? Accommodation? "Why [University]?" pages?

This determines what our tool scrapes and analyzes.

### 5. What "Good" Actually Sounds Like

Beyond catching bad language, we want the AI to recognize when a school is doing it right. Give us 2-3 examples of UK university copy that struck you as genuinely specific, differentiated, or compelling. A paragraph, a tagline, a page intro. Whatever stood out.

This helps the tool explain what "better" looks like, not just what "bad" looks like.

## Format

However you want. A shared Google Doc, an email, a voice note, a spreadsheet. The cliché list is the most critical piece. Everything else makes the tool smarter but the list is what makes it work.
